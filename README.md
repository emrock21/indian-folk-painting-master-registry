# Indian Folk Painting Traditions Master Registry

This repository preserves, in an immutable and decentralized format, the diverse folk painting traditions of India — a vast ecosystem of regional visual languages shaped by community identity, ritual practice, ecological knowledge, and ancestral memory.

The registry documents major folk painting lineages such as Madhubani, Warli, Pattachitra, Gond, Kalamkari, Cheriyal, Saura, and others. Each tradition carries its own pigments, techniques, iconographic systems, and cultural functions.

## Why Folk Painting Matters

Indian folk painting is not merely decorative art. It is:

- a **living archive** of community cosmology  
- a **ritual practice** tied to birth, marriage, harvest, and seasonal cycles  
- a **visual language** passed through families, often matrilineally  
- a **repository of ecological knowledge** (plants, animals, seasons, landscapes)  
- a **narrative system** for epics, myths, and local histories  
- a **material culture** rooted in natural pigments and indigenous technologies  

These traditions are endangered by modernization, loss of ritual context, and the disappearance of community-based transmission.

## Purpose of This Contract

The goal of this registry is to:

1. **Create a permanent, censorship-resistant record** of India’s folk painting traditions.  
2. **Standardize documentation** using structured fields that capture both technique and cultural meaning.  
3. **Preserve community knowledge** that is often oral, fragile, and geographically isolated.  
4. **Enable global public contribution** without centralized gatekeeping.  
5. **Provide a transparent, immutable reference** for museums, researchers, anthropologists, and cultural institutions.  

The contract is intentionally:

- **ownerless** — no authority can alter or censor the registry  
- **payment-free** — no monetization, no barriers  
- **minimalistic and secure** — no external calls, no complexity  
- **permanent** — entries become part of the cultural ledger  

## What Each Entry Records

Every folk painting style is documented through:

- **region** — geographic and cultural zone  
- **community or lineage** — Mithila women, Warli tribes, Chitrakar families, etc.  
- **style name** — Madhubani, Warli, Pattachitra, Gond, Kalamkari, etc.  
- **pigments** — natural dyes, rice paste, lampblack, mineral colors  
- **techniques** — mural painting, cloth preparation, dot patterns, kalam pen work  
- **iconography** — epics, rituals, deities, nature, tribal cosmology  
- **uniqueness** — what makes the tradition culturally or technically distinct  

This structure captures both the **material** and **symbolic** dimensions of folk painting.
